About me

I am an assistant professor at Korea Institute of Energy Technology (KENTECH). Before joining to KENTECH, I was a Postdoc Research Associate at Purdue University. I received a Ph.D from Network Convergence and Security Laboratory at Seoul National University. I like to design and implement cryptographic protocols appropriate to diverse networks. Also, I am leveraging machine-learning techniques for security. Currently, I am interested in various range of security including Transport Layer Security (TLS), Public Key Infrastructure (PKI), and Trusted Execution Environment (TEE) so that I will contribute to making the Internet more trustworthy and secure.
